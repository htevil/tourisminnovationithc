About the Conference

The conference addresses global challenges such as climate change, environmental degradation, and inequality, highlighting the need to move beyond mass tourism models that harm ecosystems and communities. It emphasises the link between sustainability and wellness, as modern travelers increasingly seek experiences that nurture health while supporting local communities and conserving resources.

Its mission is to bring together thought leaders, scholars, industry experts, and community stakeholders to share models, innovations, and practices shaping a more sustainable tourism future. Over three days, the conference will foster dialogue, knowledge exchange, and collaboration to create a tourism industry that promotes well-being, protects the environment, and uplifts communities worldwide.


Main Themes

  • Sustainability & Environment: Ecotourism, climate change adaptation, carbon reduction, green pilgrimages, circular economy, and eco-friendly infrastructure.
  • Wellness & Healing: Ayurveda, Yoga, holistic health, mindfulness, restorative travel, and nature-based wellness retreats.
  • Culture & Heritage : Digital storytelling, heritage conservation, community participation, indigenous wisdom, festivals, and responsible cultural tourism.
  • Community Development : Inclusive tourism, gender equity, local entrepreneurship, participatory planning, youth engagement, and accessible tourism.
  • Technology & Innovation : AR/VR experiences, green mobility, smart tourism tools, ethical digital promotion, and gamified learning.
  • Policy & Governance : SDG integration, policy innovations, research-based strategies, public private partnerships, ethics, and certification systems.
  • Solar-Powered Traveler : A Path to Carbon-Neutral
  • Wellness: Explores how solar energy directly enables a low-impact journey for the individual traveler, bridging the gap between technological innovation and personal environmental consciousness.

Conference Objectives & Format

Primary Objectives

  • Showcase innovative sustainable tourism practices.
